Ben Carson's life

  • 1951- Ben Carson was born September 18, 1951 in Detroit, Michigan, U.S. to Robert Solomon Carson Jr. and his wife, Sonya Carson.
  • 1956- He began his education at Detroit Public SChools.
  • 1973- He earned a full scholarship to Yale, receiving a B.A. degree in psychology.
  • He entered the University of Michigan Medical School.
  • 1975- He married Lacena "Candy" Rustin. The couple have three sons; Rhoeyce, Benjamin Jr., and Murray, as well as several grandchildren.
  • 1977- Ben Carson graduated from the University of Michigan Medical School with an M.D. He began internship at Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine neurosurgery program.
  • 1978- He became a neurosurgery residentat Johns Hopkins.
  • 1982- He became chief resident in neurosurgery at Hopkins
  • 1983- He received and honoured an important invitation to work at Gairdner Hospital, in Nedlands, a suburb of Perth, Western Australia as a Senior Registrar in neurosurgery.
  • 1984- Ben Carson returned to Johns Hopkins and became Director of pediatric neurosurgery at the age of 33, becoming the youngest U.S. physician to hold such a position at that time.
  • 1987- Ben Carson led a huge team of doctors, nurses and support staff to perform a surgery to separate 7-month-old occipital craniopagus twins in Germany, Patrick and Benjamin Binder who were born joined at the head. In the operation, Ben Carson had applied both hypothermic and circulatory arrest. Although the twins suffered some brain damage and post-operation bleeding, both survived the separation, allowing Ben Carson's surgery to be considered by the medical establishment the first successful procedure of its kind.
  • 1994- Ben Carson and his team went to South Africa to separate the Makwaeba twins. The operation was unsuccessful, as both girls died from complications of the surgery. Ben Carson was devastated but vowed to press on, as he knew such procedures could be successful.
  • 1994- Ben Carson and his wife, Candy Carson founded the Carson Scholars Fund that grants scholarships to students and promotes reading in the younger grades.
  • 1997- Ben Carson and his team went to Zambia in South Central Africa to separate infant boys Luka and Joseph Banda. Although the operation was difficult and kknown to be first of it's kind, it was successful and neither boys suffered brain damage.
  • 2000- The Library of Congress selected Carson as one of its "Living Legends."
  • 2001- CNN and Time magazine named Carson as one of the nation's 20 foremost physicians and scientists.
  • 2002- Ben Carson developed prostate cancer but he fully recovered from the operation cancer-free after the operation. After his recovery, he still kept a busy schedule, conducting operations and speaking to various groups around the country. He has also written several books, including the popular autobiography Gifted Hands (1990). Other titles include—Think Big (1992), The Big Picture (1999), and Take the Risk (2007)—are about his personal philosophies on learning, success, hard work and religious faith.
  • 2003- In Singapore, Southeast Asia, on July 6, 2003, Carson and his team of more than 100 surgeons, specialists and assistants began a nearly 52-hour operation to seperate adult conjoined twins, Ladan and Laleh Bijani. These Iranian women have been joined at the head for 29 years. The surgery was unsuccessful as both twins died after the operation which was devasting to all especially Ben Carson but he stated that the girls' bravery to pursue the operation had contributed to neurosurgery in ways that would live far beyond them.
  • 2006- He received the Spingarn Medal, the highest honor bestowed by the NAACP.
  • 2008- President George W. Bush awarded Carson the Ford's Theatre Lincoln Medal and the Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2009- Actor Cuba Gooding Jr. portrayed Carson in the television production Gifted Hands.
  • 2012- He published America the Beautiful: Rediscovering What Made This Nation Great
  • 2013- He attracted attention for his speech at the National Prayer Breakfast. He retired being a surgeon. He was hired by Fox News in October 2013 to work as a contributor.
  • 2014- He published his No. 1 New York Times bestseller One Nation: What We Can All Do To Save America's Future.
  • 2015- Ben Carson launched his official bid for the Republican presidential nomination at an event in Detroit on May 4, 2015.
  • 2016- He suspended his campaign on March 4 and announced he would be the new national chairman of My Faith Votes, a group that encourages Christians to exercise their civic duty to vote. On December 5, 2016, Trump announced he was nominating Carson as the secretary of the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD).
  • 2017- The Senate Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs Committee unanimously approved Carson's nomination on January 24, 2017. On March 2, 2017, Carson was confirmed by the Senate for the position of Secretary of Housing and Urban Development
